So, who am I? Great question!

"My name is Jessica, and I am a third-year Electrical Engineering student at the University of Toronto. I'm passionate about many areas of ECE, with particular interest in digital electronics, including digital systems, low-level hardware, and ASIC design.  I also enjoy working on circuit and PCB design."
Why am I in engineering in the first place? I was always drawn to it from a young age. I loved fashioning trinkets out of various household items like cardboard and tape, which I dubbed my "inventions".  Somehow, none of these ideas made it to step 8 of the inventors' checklist.

"Matthew's Monster Potion" belonged to my brother Matthew. I hope that everything else (fleeboflabs and garglebunkers alike) makes perfect sense.

I'm sure you want a visualization of who was behind these genius creations. Ask and you shall receive.

I hope that I look a little more approachable now.

As soon as I learned that "inventors" were actually engineers, I knew I needed to do that for my career. I'm a creative person, who moves between various crafts like sewing, knitting, and jewelry creation in my spare time. However, I believe that engineering is equally creative. In particular, electrical engineering is so multifaceted due to its ubiquity in our modern-day society. I'm very happy with my engineering journey so far; it's been challenging, but so interesting! Check out my "Projects" page and see what I've done.
